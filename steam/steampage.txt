[b]Better Lights[/b]

[i]Configurable dynamic lighting for Garry's Mod.[/i]

[img]https://files.catbox.moe/8uylmi.gif[/img]

[hr][/hr]

[b]Overview[/b]

Better Lights adds configurable client-side lighting to common Garry's Mod effects. It focuses on things you see during normal play, such as weapons, NPCs, pickups, projectiles, fire, explosions, and the player flashlight.

[list]
[*]Adds light to common weapons, NPCs, entities, and effects.
[*]Includes a customizable shadow casting flashlight.
[*]Lets you disable the parts you do not want.
[/list]

[img]https://files.catbox.moe/z9kpr2.gif[/img]

[hr][/hr]

[b]Settings[/b]

Open the spawn menu and go to the [b]Better Lights[/b] tab. Settings are split by feature group so you do not have to hunt through one huge page.

Most lights have controls for enable, radius, brightness, color, and placement. The flashlight has extra controls for texture, sound, tint, flicker, sway, shadows, and beam position.

Use [b]Profiles > Settings Profiles[/b] to save, load, import, and export your personal settings.

Servers can let players choose whether to use Better Lights or enforce shared flashlight settings.

[img]https://files.catbox.moe/okwsv1.gif[/img]

[hr][/hr]

[b]Engine Limits[/b]

Better Lights works inside the normal Source Engine lighting limits. These limits are shared with the map, the default flashlight, and other addons.

[list]
[*]Dynamic lights: 32 world lights and 64 entity lights can be active at once.
[*]Shadowed projected textures: 8 can be active by default across flashlights, map lights, and addon spotlights.
[*]If the limit is reached, lights may disappear, flicker, or be replaced by newer lights.
[/list]

Better Lights reserves some headroom and prioritizes its most visually significant lights when a scene gets crowded. You can adjust light budgets and optional distance fading under [b]Better Lights > General > Performance[/b].

Advanced users can raise the shadow texture limit with the launch option [b]-numshadowtextures LIMIT[/b], but more shadowed lights can reduce performance.

Busy combat, large NPC scenes, heavy particle effects, and other lighting addons can hit these limits faster. If that happens, lower radius or brightness, disable feature groups you do not need, or turn off model lights and shadows.

[hr][/hr]

[b]Compatibility[/b]

[list]
[*]Should work with most GShader Library addons.
[*]Includes compatibility for MW Base, ARC9, ArcCW, M9K, Chuck's Weaponry 2.0 (CW 2.0), and TFA Base. If another weapon base does not work with Better Lights, send the link and I can take a look at it.
[*]Most features are client-side and can be disabled individually. There are a few server-side effects such as muzzle flashes, flashlight beams and bullet impacts.
[*][url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3754051046]Re-Light - Dynamic Lights[/url] is not compatible. It does similar things that WILL overlap, so choose Better Lights or Re-Light, not both.
[/list]

Flashlight mods and other lighting mods can conflict if they affect the same flashlight, weapons, NPCs, or effects. If you report a lighting issue, please test without those addons first.

[hr][/hr]

[b]Recommended[/b]

[b]Use the 64-bit branch of Garry's Mod if you can.[/b]

Better Lights is made and tested on 64-bit, and Garry's Mod usually runs better there.
